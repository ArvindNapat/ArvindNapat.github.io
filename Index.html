Mercury

History

Being the closest to the sun, Mercury only spends 88 earth days to complete a full rotation around the sun.
Due to it's close proximity, it is seen as a morning "star" in the sky.
Due to it's composition, some believe Mercury used to have magma seas.

Classification

Type: Terrestrial
Volume: 0.056 Earths
Title: The morning "star"

Fun Fact

On avarage, mercury is the closest to all planets most of the time.

Neptune

History

Discovered in 1846, Neptune is the last planet in the solar system and is the furthest away from the sun.
Neptune's weather is extreamly dynamic with supersonic winds at speeds of around 600 meters per second.
Neptune also has 16 discovered moons as of 2025.

Classification

Type: Ice Giant
Volume: 57.74 Earths
Title: The wind planet

Fun Facts

it takes 164.8 earth years for neptune to make one rotation around the sun.
